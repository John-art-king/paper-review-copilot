# Related-Work Comparison

Compare papers by research question and mechanism before comparing numbers. A title-keyword match is not necessarily the closest prior work.

## Source Set

For a novelty or priority discussion, identify:

1. earliest clear precursor;
2. closest mechanism-level predecessor;
3. contemporary competing approach;
4. newer challenger when current relevance matters.

Prefer proceedings, publisher pages, arXiv, supplements, official code, and project pages. Record the relevant publication date and distinguish an author characterization from an independently verified comparison.

## Comparison Matrix

Normalize datasets, preprocessing, model scale, compute budget, tuning budget, metric definition, and evaluation protocol before treating results as comparable. Mark any incomparable cell instead of forcing a ranking.

## Mechanism Delta

For the reviewed paper and its closest predecessor, answer:

- What exact component, objective, constraint, or interpretation changes?
- Why should that difference change the behavior?
- Which evidence isolates the difference?
- What alternative explanation remains plausible?
- What single comparison would most strongly challenge the claimed novelty?

## Group-Meeting Close

End a comparison with:

- what the field now establishes;
- what remains disputed or regime-dependent;
- which paper has the strongest evidence for each subproblem;
- the smallest decisive experiment for the most important unresolved disagreement.
