# Group-Meeting Deck Guide

Use this guide after the analytical review is stable. A strong group meeting explains the tension, mechanism, and evidence before asking the audience to accept a novelty claim.

## Duration Presets

Choose the smallest deck that preserves the argument. Slide counts exclude title and backup slides.

| Duration | Content slides | Emphasis |
|---:|---:|---|
| 5 min | 4-5 | Question, mechanism, one decisive result, verdict |
| 10 min | 7-8 | Add closest work, experiment design, and one limitation |
| 15 min | 10-12 | Full default narrative below |
| 20 min | 12-15 | Add deeper mechanism, ablations, and discussion |
| 30 min | 15-18 | Add related-work map, failure analysis, and research agenda |

Do not fill extra time with background. Use it to explain mechanism, inspect decisive evidence, or test the verdict.

## Default 12-15 Minute Narrative

Use 10-12 content slides.

| Slide purpose | Count | Time |
|---|---:|---:|
| Field tension and research question | 1-2 | 1.5 min |
| Closest prior work and exact gap | 1 | 1.0 min |
| Core insight | 1 | 1.0 min |
| Method and causal mechanism | 2-3 | 3.5 min |
| Experimental design and decisive results | 2-3 | 3.5 min |
| Reviewer-style critique and novelty verdict | 1-2 | 2.0 min |
| Research opportunity and takeaway | 1 | 1.0 min |

## Slide Contract

For every slide, provide:

```text
Slide N - [claim-style title]
Purpose:
Primary visual:
On-slide content:
Speaker takeaway:
Evidence locators:
Transition:
```

Use one primary visual or comparison per slide. Explain axes, units, conditions, and the result's consequence. Never place an unexplained headline chart on a slide.

## Questions To Prepare

- Why is this research question important now?
- What is genuinely new relative to the closest work?
- Why should the mechanism work?
- Are the comparisons fair and the ablations decisive?
- What fails and under which regime?
- What result would overturn the main conclusion?
- Which research direction follows directly from the evidence?

Mark every answer as supported, inferred, or unknown.

## Final Quality Gate

Before delivery, check that the audience can state the problem by slide 2, the closest prior work appears before the novelty claim, the method has a causal explanation, results include conditions, critique separates fatal flaws from normal limitations, and the final slide presents a verdict instead of a generic thank-you message.
