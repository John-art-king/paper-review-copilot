# Group-Meeting Deliverable Contract

Use this contract to keep deep reviews and meeting packs consistent. Compress sections for short presentations; do not silently omit uncertainty.

## 1. Review Profile

Record language, audience, duration, depth, stance, paper identity, venue and year when verified, and available sources.

## 2. Executive Verdict

Write six items before drafting slides:

1. research question in one sentence;
2. core insight in one sentence;
3. mechanism in one compact causal chain;
4. strongest evidence with locator;
5. largest unresolved threat;
6. calibrated read, discuss, or build-on verdict.

## 3. Argument Map

Use this sequence:

`field tension -> closest prior limitation -> paper's intervention -> mechanism -> predicted effect -> observed evidence -> justified conclusion`

Mark any broken link. A result can support effectiveness without isolating the claimed mechanism.

## 4. Claim-Evidence Ledger

| Claim | Status | Locator | What the evidence establishes | What it does not establish | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Keep author claims and reviewer conclusions in separate rows when they differ.

## 5. Contribution And Rigor Verdict

Summarize:

- closest mechanism-level predecessor and exact delta;
- importance and technical substance;
- baseline, budget, and protocol fairness;
- decisive ablations and missing controls;
- generality, failure modes, and reproducibility;
- one calibrated verdict from the top-venue rubric.

## 6. Timed Presentation

Provide a slide-by-slide plan using the group-meeting deck guide. Include cumulative time so the script can be rehearsed. For every slide, identify the claim, primary visual, evidence locator, speaker takeaway, and transition.

## 7. Discussion Kit

Provide:

- three questions for the audience;
- five likely advisor questions;
- one strongest defense and one strongest criticism;
- two or three falsifiable follow-up ideas;
- a 30-second final takeaway.

## 8. Confidence Footer

Close with:

- sources inspected;
- sources unavailable;
- conclusions most sensitive to missing evidence;
- claims that should be verified before public reuse.
